# 15 — Conversion & competitor research (2026-07-20)

Fresh, source-backed research into what actually makes a pet DTC store convert, and
how the top competitors do it. Prepared this session via live web search (the
storefront + competitor sites remain firewalled from this environment, so
competitor *site* specifics are from public search results, not live inspection).
Figures come largely from **secondary aggregators reporting industry studies** —
directional and consistent across sources, but confirm at the primary source before
quoting externally. No statistic here is invented.

---

## A. Competitor teardown

### My Anxious Dog (myanxiousdog.co.uk)
- **Position:** ruthlessly narrow — "**space awareness**" for nervous/reactive dogs.
  Yellow harnesses, leads, collars, lead covers with embroidered warning messaging
  ("NERVOUS", "GIVE ME SPACE"). UK-designed & tested, award-winning.
- **Proof they use:** owner-outcome stats — *"78% of owners who walk their
  #dogsinyellow enjoy walks more / feel calmer; 66% say it reduced encounters."*
- **What they do that wins:** (1) a single, findable niche; (2) a **movement**
  (#dogsinyellow) that turns customers into marketers; (3) durability/quality proof
  (embroidered not printed, machine washable); (4) outcome-framed proof, not just star
  ratings.
- **Lesson for Dog Nook:** own a lane, frame proof as *owner outcomes* ("owners tell
  us their dog settles faster"), and give buyers a cause to belong to.

### Lords & Labradors (lordsandlabradors.co.uk)
- **Position:** premium curator. Its **"Anxious Pet"** collection curates recognised
  calming brands (Pet Remedy sprays/diffusers/wipes) **plus its own calming
  anti-anxiety donut beds**.
- **Free delivery over £75**; next-day available. Also distributed **through Pets at
  Home** (the trusted incumbent) — so they own the "calming donut bed" search term
  and the premium shelf.
- **Lesson for Dog Nook:** L&L is your most direct bed competitor and it's *premium*
  (£75 free-ship). Your opening: **more generous free-ship (£35), honesty, and a
  keener price** — but you must match them on **imagery**, which is where they're
  strong and you're currently empty.

### FunnyFuzzy (funnyfuzzy.co.uk)
- **Position:** DTC pet *lifestyle* brand (top-200 global pet brand, 150 countries).
- **What they do that wins:** rich **lifestyle photography + video**, an education
  content hub, and **UGC** (#MyFunnyFuzzyMoments). Heavy **email automation**:
  reported **+40% conversion**, **70% email open rate**, **59% of email revenue from
  automation**, and they **doubled AOV** after adding Shopify Flow.
- **Their weakness (your opening):** public reviews are **mixed on order tracking,
  billing transparency, support responsiveness and quality-for-price**.
- **Lesson for Dog Nook:** copy the *content + UGC + email-automation + AOV* playbook;
  **attack their weakness** — your "a real person replies," honest delivery, and
  30-day guarantee are exactly the trust gaps their buyers complain about.

### Pets at Home (petsathome.com)
- The trusted UK incumbent and the **"safe default"** shoppers compare an unknown
  brand against. It stocks L&L's beds. You don't beat it on breadth or trust — you
  beat it on **niche focus + a personal, honest brand**.

## B. What the evidence says actually converts (with sources)

### 1. Photography is the #1 lever (and your #1 gap)
- **56%** of shoppers' *first action* on a product page is to explore the images —
  before title, description or price. **67%** cite **image quality as the top factor**
  in their buying decision, ahead of description, reviews and price. **28%** of sites
  still lack an **"in-scale"** image. (Baymard)
- Visually appealing sites have a **38% lower bounce rate** than text-heavy ones.
- **Implication:** with **zero images**, The Dog Nook is failing the single most
  decisive PDP moment for two-thirds of shoppers. Nothing else on this list
  outweighs it. Priority: real photos, incl. **one in-scale shot per product**,
  **Calming Donut Bed first** (highest price, most image-dependent).

### 2. Video is the second lever (once photos exist)
- Product pages **with video: +37% add-to-cart**, and viewers are **up to 144% more
  likely to add to cart**; some studies show up to **80% higher conversion** for
  in-use video.
- **Implication:** a short clip of a dog using the snuffle/lick mat or settling into
  the bed is a large, cheap lever after stills exist.

### 3. Reviews — you now have them, and they matter most at your higher price points
- **93–98%** of consumers consult reviews. Displaying them lifts sales **~19.8%**
  (commonly cited **10–30%** range). A product with **5 reviews is ~270% more likely
  to sell** than one with none; **11–30 reviews convert ~68% higher** than zero.
  **Verified-buyer** reviews add **~15%**.
- Critically: the lift is **bigger on higher-priced products** (one study: **+380%**
  on a higher-priced item vs **+190%** on a low-priced one).
- **Implication:** your new **4.8 / 80-review** wall + PDP stars are on-strategy, and
  they matter *most* for the **Donut Bed and the bundles** (your priciest items).

### 4. Bundles & AOV — validated, and you're doing it right
- Bundling lifts **AOV by 20–35%** (McKinsey via Swell). Offering **bundles *and*
  individual items** generates **25–35% more revenue** than bundles alone. Cross-sell
  can be **up to 30% of ecommerce revenue**. Selling to existing customers is **5–25×
  cheaper** than acquiring new ones.
- **Implication:** the **Rescue Bundle (entry) + new Settle-In Bundle (premium)** two-
  tier structure is exactly right. Next AOV levers: **cart cross-sell** and
  **post-purchase upsell** (app), plus **subscriptions on consumables** (mats, lick
  refills) — a lever every competitor uses and you don't yet.

### 5. Free-shipping threshold — a tuning opportunity
- Best practice: set the threshold **15–30% above your AOV**; it lifts AOV **12–24%**
  because **~58%** of shoppers add an item to qualify. Set it *at* AOV and you just
  give away margin.
- **Implication:** your **£35** is generous and trust-building (vs L&L's £75) — keep
  it for launch. Once you have real AOV data, if AOV settles around £30–35, a
  **£40–45** threshold likely lifts basket size. Revisit with data, don't guess now.

### 6. Email capture / first-order offer — your 10% is in the sweet spot
- Discount popups convert **~2.4%** vs **1.7%** without an offer; ecommerce best
  reaches **~6.88%**. **"X% off your first order"** specificity beats vague "special
  offer" by **40–60%**. **10–15%** is the sweet-spot discount (below 10% feels
  nothing; above 15% erodes margin). Gamified "spin-to-win" popups convert **8–15%**
  — but risk the pushy/dropshipper feel (see §7).
- **Implication:** **SETTLE10 (10% off first order)** is textbook-correct. Keep the
  popup clean rather than gamified — trust matters more for this brand than the extra
  points a spin-wheel buys.

### 7. Trust signals — and hard proof the fake tactics *backfire*
- What builds trust: **reviews near the Add-to-Cart**, **transparent costs + delivery
  up front**, **fast load (<2.5s; 53% of mobile users abandon slower)**, and a
  **mobile-first** layout (**62%** of ecommerce traffic is mobile).
- **Direct finding:** *"real-time customer activity, countdown timers… **'542 people
  viewing this item right now'** feels like a high-pressure sales tactic straight from
  a dropshipping template"* — it **increases mistrust and bounce**.
- **Implication:** this is independent, conversion-side confirmation that the fake
  urgency/scarcity/viewer-count tactics **lose money for this brand** — not just a
  legal risk (UK DMCC 2024, CMA fines to 10% turnover), but a *conversion* mistake.
  Your honest positioning is the optimisation, not a constraint on it.

### 8. Benchmarks to judge yourself against (once live)
- Shopify average conversion **~1.4–3%** (top 10% >4.7%); **pet supplies ~2.45–3.28%**,
  pet care ~4.4%. **Pet AOV ~$59 (~£45)**. Pet cart abandonment **~53%**.
- **Implication:** target **≥2.5%** once photos + reviews are live; treat anything
  under ~1.5% as a signal the imagery/trust layer still isn't landing.

**Bottom line:** the store's *positioning, copy, trust framing, reviews and offer
stack now match or beat the winners.* The one decisive gap is **imagery** (#1–#2 +
#6). Photos + publishing the theme unlock more conversion than every other item on
this list combined — and the research confirms the honest path (no fake urgency)
is the higher-converting one, not a handicap.
