# 15 — Product expansion action plan

> Companion to `13-prioritised-action-plan.md`. That plan fixes the *store*;
> this one grows the *range*. Every pick is judged against the brand's five gates
> (see below), not just "does it sell."
>
> **Created:** 2026-07-20

## The five gates (a product must pass all five)
1. **On-narrative** — solves an *anxiety / rescue / calm-home* moment (fireworks,
   storms, separation, settling a rescue, grooming dread, car stress). Generic pet
   gadgets dilute the positioning.
2. **Dropship-shippable** — light, unbreakable, neutral packaging, no battery/liquid
   customs headache. (Beds are already the worst shippers — don't add more bulk.)
3. **Margin** — target **≥60% gross**.
4. **No medical/regulatory landmine** — honesty is the brand's edge. No supplements,
   tablets, pheromones, or "cure"/behavioural claims (DMCC 2024 / CMA; own rules).
5. **AOV fit** — priced to push over the £35 free-ship line and to bundle.

|---|---|
| Calming supplements / tablets (Zylkene, YuMOVE-type) | Medical claims + consumable import/expiry/returns risk. Off-brand + legal risk. |

| Calming caves / hooded beds / booster "den" beds | Right narrative, but bulky + slow shipping wrecks margin and the delivery-honesty promise. |

## The honest caveat (don't skip)
More SKUs will **not** fix the store's real #1 conversion blocker — **no real
photography and no real reviews** (see `13-...` P1-1/P1-3). New products convert far
better *after* they have warm in-use shots. If forced to choose one hour: photography
first. This plan is the right *catalogue* move; it is not a substitute for the trust layer.
