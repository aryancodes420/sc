## What The Dog Nook already does *better* than a typical dropshipper
- Own-branded, curated range with a coherent story (vs endless catalogue). **[Obs]**
- Explicitly **refuses fake reviews and fake urgency** — an honest posture that is
  now also the legally safe one (S17–S18). **[Obs]**
- A cleanly engineered, fast, accessible-leaning custom theme. **[Obs]**

## Where it is clearly behind
- **Imagery and proof.** Every observed competitor shows real products and real
  ratings; The Dog Nook shows neither. This is the decisive gap. **[Comp][Obs]**
- **Company legitimacy signals** (identity, populated socials). **[Obs]**

## Takeaway
The brand doesn't need to out-spend incumbents; it needs to **look as real as it
claims to be**. The nearest comparables (My Anxious Dog, L&L's Anxious Pet) prove
the *positioning* is viable — they've built discovery and trust around exactly this
customer. The Dog Nook has the positioning and the theme; it is missing the
evidence layer those competitors lead with.
