**Summary:** the positioning *messaging* is above average; the *substantiation* is
largely missing. The gap between what the copy promises (a trusted UK specialist)
and what the evidence shows (no images, no reviews, placeholder identity, uncertain
delivery) is the central commercial problem.

## Positioning ↔ product/experience mismatches (flag list)
- **Anxiety positioning vs generic feeding/travel SKUs.** The Car Boot Liner and,
  arguably, the Slow-Feeder Bowl and Nail Grinder are practical products only
  loosely tied to *anxiety*. They fit an "enrichment & calm home" umbrella but
  dilute a pure "anxious dog" promise. **[Obs][Judg]** — keep, but merchandise
  under a benefit ("calmer mealtimes", "stress-free grooming", "calmer car trips")
  rather than leaving them looking like a random catalogue.
- **Invented product/brand names over generic goods.** All products are renamed
  ("The Lick Mat", "The New Rescue Bundle", vendor "The Dog Nook") over what are
  generic CJdropshipping items. This is legitimate own-branding **only if** the
  product that arrives isn't in conflicting third-party packaging. **Flag [Verify]:**
  confirm delivered items are unbranded/neutral; mismatched packaging is a major
  trust breaker.
- **"Made in the UK" (footer) vs dropshipping model.** "Made in the UK" is very
  likely **inaccurate** for CJ-sourced goods and is a factual claim that could
  breach unfair-practices rules (S17). **[Obs]** Change to something true (e.g.
  "A UK business" / "Run from the UK") — do not claim UK manufacture unless true.
- **Bundle facts differ by page** (see `data/live-store-facts.md`). **[Obs]**

## Recommended positioning kit (proposal, not yet implemented)
- **Ideal customer profile:** UK dog owner (often of a rescue or a nervous/reactive
  dog) who feels responsible for their dog's distress around fireworks, storms,
  separation, grooming or car travel, and wants *practical, non-medical* ways to
  help — and who is wary of cheap dropshipped tat and cares about buying from a
  real, honest UK seller. **[Judg]**
- **Core customer problem:** "My dog gets anxious/overstimulated and I don't know
  what actually helps — and I don't want to waste money on junk."
- **Brand promise:** "A small, honest UK shop of calm, curated kit for dogs who
  need a little extra reassurance — with the risk on us, not you."
- **Value proposition:** Curated (not catalogue) · honest (no fake reviews, no fake
  urgency, real delivery times) · low-risk (30-day guarantee) · reachable (a real
  person replies).
- **Primary homepage headline (keep):** "Less anxious. More at home."
- **Supporting subheadline (tighten):** "Calming beds, mats and gentle grooming kit,
  hand-picked for anxious and rescue dogs — chosen by a small UK team, guaranteed
  for 30 days."
- **Three principal buying reasons:** (1) Curated for anxious/rescue dogs, not a
  random catalogue; (2) 30-day money-back — the risk is ours; (3) A real UK team
  that replies, with honest delivery times.
- **Brand voice:** gentle, plain-spoken, low-pressure, quietly expert; never hyped,
  never medical, never fake-urgent. (Already largely achieved in copy.)
- **Evidence standard for claims:** no health/medical/behavioural cure claims;
  comfort/enrichment framing only; no review or rating shown until it is real and
  verifiable (S18); delivery and origin stated truthfully (S17).
