## Naming logic
- The **problem** is in the name (First Days / Fireworks / Home-Alone / Calm).
- The **tier** is the noun: **Kit** = focused starter · **Bundle** = signature · **System** = complete flagship.
- Fireworks is a deliberate **2-tier ladder** (Ready £39.99 → Survival £84.99) — your biggest season, worth catching both wallets.

## Competitor-benchmark price adjustments (2026-07-24)
Ran UK market benchmarking (see `audit/competitor-price-benchmark.md`). Changes:
- **Auto-Play Ball £29.99 → £24.99** — was priced *above* the branded PetDroid (£23.99 UK).
- **Deep Nook bed XXL £99.99 → £89.99** — £99.99 was top-of-market; £89.99 still premium.
- **The Home-Alone Kit £74.99 → £69.99** — follows the ball drop; keeps the saving honest.
- Kept: Heartbeat £24.99 (vs Snuggle Puppy £34.99) and Calming Coat £24.99 (vs ThunderShirt £30–40) —
  both deliberately undercut the brand names (honest-value positioning). Room to £29.99 later if wanted.
- **Bundle concept validated:** competitors sell anxiety kits (Snuggle Puppy "New Puppy All-in-One Kit",
  L&L "Puppy Starter Packs") — bundling is a proven play, not a guess.
